My story

A few words about my education and work experience

Once my brother, who is a successful programmer now, said that linguists may become excellent coders and QAs, but I thought that it’s definitely not my cup of tea. So, I chose another path. Now I’m 35 years old, and I’m an Associate Professor at the Language Training Department of the National University of Civil Protection of Ukraine (Kharkiv). I joined the department in 2012 after getting my Master’s degrees in Ukrainian Philology and English translation and PhD in Ukrainian literature. I also has been successfully working as a translator, editor and proofreader for main Kharkiv publishing houses for the last 15 years. In 2017 I passed FCE and proved C1 level (my total score was 181). From June 2022 I has been working as a remote translator for Digi Media Ltd. Also now I’m on a childcare leave until my daughter reaches the age of 3, and I want to use the free time as much effective as possible and gain new professional knowledge and skills. I understand that I’ve reached my ceiling in career growth and I ready to try a new path. Furthermore, I believe that it’s a right decision due to the crisis caused by the war. And I’m sure that my inspiration, industriousness, openness to new, accuracy, mindfulness and analytical skills will help me to become a good specialist in the field of IT. That’s why the grant is a great opportunity for me, and I find the course program very interesting.
